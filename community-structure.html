Community of Practice (CoP) Structure

The Community of Practice (CoP) is chartered by ADAC to foster federated cooperation among analytics groups across the enterprise. It serves as the central hub for knowledge sharing, standards development, and collaborative innovation, connecting operational Centers of Excellence with specialized Clinical Analytics Hubs.

Purpose & Responsibilities

  • Foster federated cooperation among Centers of Excellence and Clinical Analytics Hubs
  • Act as an agent to de-silo data products across the organization
  • Promote enterprise-level data & analytics products and shared definitions
  • Maintain Analytics Central as a centralized resource hub
  • Create and maintain data analytics publishing standards and versioning practices
  • Promote data literacy and self-service analytics across the organization

Membership & Structure

Leadership Team

  • Conrad Gleber, MD, MBA — Facilitator & Moderator
  • Primary contact from each CoE and Clinical Analytics Hub

General Membership

  • At least one operational lead from each CoE/CAH
  • Additional team members from each CoE/CAH

Understanding Centers of Excellence & Clinical Analytics Hubs

Our federated analytics structure distinguishes between operational Centers of Excellence and specialty-focused Clinical Analytics Hubs, recognizing their unique purposes while enabling collaboration through our Community of Practice.

Centers of Excellence (CoEs)

  • Primary focus: Enterprise-wide operational functions
  • Scope: Service-oriented support for the entire organization
  • Data focus: Generalized operational metrics and KPIs
  • Expertise: Broad domain knowledge applicable across departments
  • Examples: Finance, Quality Improvement, Information Systems
  • Responsibility: Creating and maintaining analytics products within their area of expertise for the entire enterprise

Clinical Analytics Hubs (CAHs)

  • Primary focus: Specific clinical specialty or service line
  • Scope: Deep, specialized support for clinical decision-making
  • Data focus: Clinical outcomes, research insights, patient care metrics
  • Expertise: Specialized clinical knowledge with integrated analytics
  • Examples: Oncology, Neurosurgery, Cardiology
  • Responsibility: Translating analytics into clinical practice improvements and research advances specific to their domain
Attribute Centers of Excellence Clinical Analytics Hubs
Organizational focus Horizontal (across departments) Vertical (within specialty)
Primary stakeholders Executive leadership, operations teams Clinical leaders, physicians, researchers
Analytics application Operational efficiency, business processes Clinical outcomes, patient care quality, research
Data sources Enterprise systems, financial platforms EHR clinical data, specialty registries, research databases
Decision support Strategic and operational decisions Clinical practice and research decisions
Governance relationship Direct reporting to ADAC Collaborating with ADAC through the Community of Practice

Federated Development Model

Our federated development model balances centralized governance with distributed expertise, enabling scalable analytics capabilities while maintaining enterprise standards and security.

  • Enterprise platform management
  • Data integration architecture
  • Security and compliance
  • Standards and guidelines
  • Governance facilitation
  • Training and enablement

Departmental Analytics Teams

  • Subject matter expertise
  • Local data stewardship

Getting Started with Analytics Collaboration

For Clinical Departments

Immediate Actions

  1. Identify your department's current analytics capabilities and needs
  2. Review the Clinical Analytics Hub application template
  3. Designate potential leadership for your analytics team
  4. Document key use cases that would benefit from enhanced analytics
  5. Contact ADAC facilitators to express interest in joining the Community of Practice

Resources Available

  • Clinical Analytics Hub application template
  • Community of Practice Charter
  • Consultation with existing CoE leaders
  • Analytics Central portal resources
  • ADAC advisory support

For Operational Departments

Immediate Actions

  1. Evaluate opportunities to establish or formalize a Center of Excellence
  2. Identify enterprise-wide data and analytics needs in your domain
  3. Review the CoE application and charter template
  4. Connect with existing CoE leaders to understand best practices
  5. Develop a strategic plan for your analytics initiatives

Resources Available

  • Center of Excellence application template
  • Enterprise analytics architecture documentation
  • Data governance framework
  • Development standards and templates
  • Training resources for analytics technologies

Next Steps for All Participants

  1. Register for the next Community of Practice quarterly meeting
  2. Explore resources on the Analytics Central portal
  3. Identify potential contributions to the shared knowledge base
  4. Connect with peers in other departments to explore collaboration opportunities
  5. Participate in upcoming training and enablement sessions
